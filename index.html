ScaleGreens

ScaleGreens

aims to produce plants and vegetables in very high volumes with minimum exploitation of resources like land, water, and soil. Our focus is to grow excellent ornamental plants and produce high-quality fruits and vegetables. We will leverage state-of-the-art plant tissue culture technology for large-scale propagation.

About ScaleGreens

When Manoj transitioned from the US to India, back in 2018, he was looking for a place to stay in Delhi. Within a few months, he realised that cities like Delhi had become a concrete jungle. There were hardly any places left for green cover, and high pollution levels made Delhi air toxic to breathe.

Finally,

he found a small flat near Chattarpur farms, and that's where the idea of ScaleGreens started. First, it was out of his passion for plants, but then the notion expanded to optimise the indoor environment, reduce pollution, and help with the cooling load at home.

With ScaleGreens in mind,

Manoj propagated several plant and tree species at his home and IIT campuses and started learning about plant hormones and tissue culture techniques.

He put together a team of like-minded people who could help in acquiring and developing state-of-the-art biotech skills and make this a profitable venture that could create more jobs. We want to leave this world a little better than how we found it.
That is how ScaleGreens came into being, and this is our story.

Research and Development

Plant Tissue Culture

It is a well established in-vitro technique to mass propagate plant species and can be leveraged extensively to have several replicas of a healthy, disease free and highly productive plant.

Vertical Farming

It is an amazing technique to increase yield per hectare of land by stacking plants and vegetable racks vertically. It can increase production by 10x to 20x in comparison to conventional horizontal farming techniques.

Drip Irrigation

This technique initially started in deserted lands to facilitate agriculture, but is most widely used now to improve water efficiency in irrigation. It can significantly optimise water consumption while still ensuring similar yields.
